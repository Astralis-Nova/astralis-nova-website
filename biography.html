Ramon Bivens
Astralis Nova

Technician, father, music-maker, world-builder, and lifelong explorer of the space where memory, technology, humor, and imagination meet.

A new star arising

More than an artist name

Astralis Nova is the creative universe of Ramon Bivens. The name carries the idea of a new star arising, something shaped by the past but still moving toward unexplored space. Ramon does not separate life into neat little compartments. Music, technology, family, desert nights, old games, strange ideas, hard lessons, and laughter all share the same orbit here.

His story began in Vietnam and unfolded around American military bases before eventually finding a home beneath the wide Arizona sky. That journey helped build a mind that is comfortable between worlds, practical but imaginative, nostalgic but always looking ahead.

Hands-on by nature

Technology, tools, and solving the real problem

Long before Astralis Nova became a musical home, Ramon built a career in technical and semiconductor manufacturing. He has spent years around cleanrooms, wafers, production tools, robotic systems, inspections, maintenance, and the thousand small details that keep complicated operations alive.

He is the kind of problem solver who wants to understand what the machine is actually doing, not simply repeat what the manual says. That same instinct appears throughout Astralis Nova. Every page, song, animated ship, preserved MIDI file, and interactive experiment began with a simple question: could this become something real?

The emotional engine

Family, memory, and music

Ramon is a father and a deeply family-oriented person. Many of his songs began as a way to hold onto moments that time would otherwise carry away. Love, childhood, grief, relationships, family history, and the people who leave permanent fingerprints on our lives all find their way into the music.

The goal is not perfection or celebrity. It is preservation. A song can become a small time capsule, keeping a voice, a feeling, or a chapter of life glowing after the moment itself has passed.

Home coordinates

The Arizona desert after dark

The Sonoran Desert is one of the quiet co-authors of Astralis Nova. Cactus silhouettes, moonlit dirt roads, coyotes in the distance, open land, and a sky crowded with stars shape the atmosphere of the project.

Rural life keeps Ramon grounded. Animals, chickens, gardening, homestead projects, and the constant negotiation between desert beauty and desert stubbornness provide both inspiration and excellent material for the occasional cosmic chuckle.

Old worlds, new frontiers

Games, science fiction, the early web, and Nova

Ramon's imagination was fed by science fiction, space exploration, Star Trek, gaming, and virtual worlds such as Asheron's Call. He still values the handmade character of the early internet, when personal pages, MIDI music, guestbooks, and curious little portals made the web feel less like a shopping mall and more like an unexplored neighborhood.

That spirit continues through his work with local AI and Nova, an evolving offline creative assistant. For Ramon, technology is most exciting when it helps preserve history, build living worlds, support creativity, and give ordinary people the power to make extraordinary things.
